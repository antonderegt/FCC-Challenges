Ada, Countess of Lovelace
The first computer programmer

Countess of Lovelace in her pink dress, ready to program.

Time line of Ada's life:

  • 1815: Born at December 10th.
  • 1835: She married William King.
  • 1844: She commented to a friend Woronzow Greig about her desire to create a mathematical model for how the brain gives rise to thoughts and nerves to feelings ("a calculus of the nervous system").
  • 1852: Died at November 27th, she died of uterine cancer at the age of 36.

Babbage was so impressed with Lovelace's contributions, he dubbed her "The Enchantress of Numbers."
